Margaritas and Mexican Food Date Night

Craft Cocktails and Fine Dining vs. DIY Drinks and a Taco Truck Picnic

We recently went on two fun margarita and Mexican food excursions, one highbrow and one lowbrow. Both were fun and both featured bars, restraunts, or food trucks we would reccommend. We reviewed both so we could tell you what's worth it and what's not.

Fancy, or fun with a dash of DIY, both were a good time

Drinks

Maxine's Taproom

Price $24

Maxine's is pricey but worth it if you enjoy a good cocktail. This bar is one of the best in NWA and has attracted national attention. Their menu features an impressive list of classics and a rotating selection of seasonal cocktails to choose from. We opted for a Mezcal Margarita and a Paloma to go with our theme and were pleased with our beverages. Go early if you want a table or a seat at the bar.

Bottomline: Worth it when you want to splurge

DIY Margaritas

Price $6

Margaritas batch nicely to put in your thermos with some ice. Below is my recipe for one Margarita. Scale up for more servings.

  • 1.5 oz tequila
  • 1 oz Cointreau
  • 1 oz lime juice
Squeeze the limes, measure all ingredients into a shaker or jar with a lid and shake to combine. Pour over ice or into a container to hold for later.

Bottomline: cheap and fun

Dinner

Tula

Price $50

Tula is a new restaurant on the Fayetteville square specializing in higher end Mexican cuisine. Its menu features items not found on your usual Tex-Mex restaurant menu. It has nice atmosphere and is suitable for a date night without being too stuffy.

Bottomline: Interesting menu, quality food, nice atmosphere. do it if you want upscale

Lake Fayetteville Taco Trucks

Price $15

This was a solid taco. I always go for an Al Pastor, and while there are better in NWA this one is good. This was exactly what you want in a taco for an incredible price and after supper you can take a bike ride around Lake Fayetteville and come back for dessert.

Bottomline: cheap and fun is always a winner

Dessert

Tula

Price $10

We had the churros and they were tasty, but I didn't think that they were worth the price. I have heard good things about their flan and maybe I'll try that nextime. For now, I say save your money and walk down to Dickson for an icecream at Burton's Creamery or a coffee at Puritain.

Bottomline: Expensive but just O.K.

Raspados at Lake Fayetteville Food Trucks

Price $5

These were cheap and refreshing to eat outside after a bike ride around Lake Fayetteville. The selection of fruit flavors was impressive and they were delicious.

Bottomline: fun and refreshing
